## CLINICAL HISTORY

Mr. Rodriguez is a 34-year-old concrete finisher who sustained a significant lumbar spine injury during a workplace accident. According to the patient's account, he was operating a gas-powered concrete vibrator when the equipment malfunctioned, causing sudden jerking motion that led to loss of balance. He fell backward, striking his lower back on exposed rebar before landing on a concrete surface.

The patient reported immediate onset of severe lower back pain rated 8/10, inability to bear weight, muscle spasms, and numbness in his right lower extremity. He was transported via emergency medical services to Cedar Park Regional Medical Center, where initial diagnostic workup was performed.

## INITIAL DIAGNOSTIC FINDINGS

Emergency department evaluation included:

**CT Scan of Lumbar Spine (March 14, 2024):**
- L2 vertebral body compression fracture with approximately 25% anterior height loss
- No evidence of retropulsion or significant canal compromise
- Intact posterior elements at L2 level

**MRI Lumbar Spine with Contrast (March 15, 2024):**
- Confirmed L2 compression fracture with trabecular disruption
- Mild disc bulging at L1-L2 level without significant herniation
- No evidence of spinal cord compression or cauda equina syndrome
- Minimal surrounding soft tissue edema

## PHYSICAL EXAMINATION (March 18, 2024)

**General Appearance:** Well-developed male in mild to moderate distress, ambulating with careful, guarded gait

**Vital Signs:** BP 142/88, HR 76, RR 16, Temp 98.6°F

**Musculoskeletal Examination:**
- **Inspection:** TLSO brace in place, no obvious deformity

**Functional Assessment:**
- Unable to lift greater than 10 pounds

- Requires assistance with activities of daily living

## TREATMENT PLAN

**Current Management:**
1. **Bracing:** Continued use of thoracolumbar spinal orthosis (TLSO) for 8-12 weeks to maintain spinal alignment and promote fracture healing
2. **Pain Management:** 
   - Acetaminophen 1000mg TID
   - Ibuprofen 600mg TID with meals
   - Cyclobenzaprine 10mg QHS for muscle spasm
3. **Activity Modification:** No lifting >10 lbs, no bending/twisting, no prolonged sitting/standing
4. **Follow-up Imaging:** Repeat X-rays at 6 and 12 weeks to assess fracture healing

**Future Treatment Phases:**
- **Weeks 8-12:** Gradual weaning from brace based on imaging and clinical progress
- **Physical Therapy:** Initiation at 8-10 weeks post-injury focusing on:
  - Core strengthening
  - Postural re-education
  - Gradual functional restoration
  - Work conditioning program

**Surgical Consideration:** Not indicated at this time. The fracture pattern and degree of compression do not meet criteria for surgical intervention. Conservative management is appropriate and expected to result in successful healing.

## PROGNOSIS AND RETURN TO WORK ASSESSMENT

**Short-term Prognosis (0-3 months):**
Mr. Rodriguez will remain totally disabled from his regular occupation as a concrete finisher during the acute healing phase. His work requires heavy lifting (50+ lbs), prolonged standing, bending, and use of vibrating equipment - all contraindicated with his current injury.

**Medium-term Prognosis (3-6 months):**
With appropriate conservative treatment, I anticipate gradual improvement in pain levels and functional capacity. Return to modified duty with significant restrictions may be possible by 12-16 weeks post-injury, provided fracture healing progresses normally.

**Long-term Prognosis (6-12 months):**
Full return to unrestricted heavy construction work is anticipated by 6-9 months post-injury, assuming optimal healing and successful completion of work conditioning program. Some permanent restrictions regarding repetitive heavy lifting may be necessary.

**Permanent Impairment:** Based on current clinical findings and expected healing, permanent impairment rating is estimated at 8-12% whole person impairment under AMA Guidelines, 6th Edition.

## MEDICAL NECESSITY AND CAUSATION

**Relationship to Work Injury:** The L2 compression fracture is directly related to the workplace trauma sustained on March 14, 2024. The mechanism of injury (fall onto rebar with impact to lumbar spine) is entirely consistent with the radiographic findings and clinical presentation.

**Treatment Appropriateness:** All current and proposed treatments are medically necessary and appropriate for this injury. The conservative approach is the standard of care for stable compression fractures without neurological compromise.

**Alternative Treatments:** No reasonable alternative treatments exist for this condition. Surgical intervention would only be considered if conservative management fails or if neurological complications develop.

## WORK RESTRICTIONS

- No bending, twisting, or stooping
- No prolonged sitting >30 minutes

**Anticipated Modified Duty (May 15 - August 15, 2024):**
