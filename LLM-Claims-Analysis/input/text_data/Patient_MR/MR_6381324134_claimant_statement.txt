# Claimant Statement

**Claim Number:** 2024-MC-087432  
**Claimant:** Marcus J. Rodriguez  
**Date:** March 28, 2024  
**Location:** Home residence  

---

**WRITTEN STATEMENT BY CLAIMANT**

My name is Marcus Rodriguez and I am writing this statement about what happened to me at work on March 14, 2024. I want to make sure I tell you everything I can remember about the accident because it has really changed my life and my family's situation.

I have been working for Hillside Construction for eight years now as a concrete finisher. It's good work and I take pride in doing it right. I've always been careful on the job site and never had any serious injuries before, just the usual bumps and scrapes that come with construction work. Back in 2019 I strained my back a little bit but it was nothing serious and got better on its own. I did have shoulder surgery in 2021 for a torn rotator cuff but that healed up completely and never gave me any more trouble.

On March 14th, we were working at the Westfield Commercial Development site on Industrial Parkway. It was a beautiful day, clear skies and about 78 degrees. We had just finished pouring a big concrete slab and I was doing the vibrating to get all the air bubbles out. This is something I've done thousands of times before - you use this gas-powered concrete vibrator to make sure the concrete sets properly without any voids.

I was using our CV-4000 vibrator, which is a pretty standard piece of equipment that we use all the time. Everything was going normal until around 2:15 in the afternoon. I was working along the edge of the slab when all of a sudden the vibrator just went crazy. It started jerking really hard and violent, like it got stuck on something or the engine was seizing up. The resistance was so sudden and powerful that it threw me completely off balance.

I tried to keep control of the machine but there was no way. The jerking motion pulled me backward and I lost my footing. As I was falling, I remember seeing the exposed rebar sticking up behind me and thinking "oh no, this is going to be bad." I hit the rebar right in my lower back with a lot of force, and then I crashed down hard on the concrete slab.

The pain was unbelievable. I mean, I've been hurt before working construction, but this was different. It felt like someone had hit me in the back with a sledgehammer and then ran electricity through my spine. I couldn't move at first because the pain was so intense. My right leg started going numb and tingly, which really scared me.

James Patterson, our foreman, saw the whole thing happen and came running over right away. Carlos Mendez was working nearby too and he saw it. Sarah Kim, who is our safety inspector, heard the commotion and came over fast. They were all really concerned and James immediately called 911. I was trying to tell them I was okay, but honestly I couldn't even sit up. The pain in my lower back was terrible and I was getting scared about my leg going numb.

I know there were supposed to be caps on that rebar, but we were still working on getting the concrete perfect before putting them on. That's usually how we do it - finish the concrete work first, then put the safety caps on. I guess I never really thought about how dangerous those sharp pieces of rebar could be until I landed on one.

The paramedics got there pretty quick and they were really professional. They put me on a backboard and gave me some pain medication before loading me in the ambulance. The ride to Cedar Park Regional Medical Center felt like it took forever, but they took good care of me.

At the hospital, they did a bunch of tests - CT scans and MRI and X-rays. The doctor, Dr. Chen, told me I had a compression fracture in my L2 vertebra, which is in my lower back. She said about 25% of that vertebra got crushed when I hit the rebar. They also said I had some bruising on my right side and some scrapes on my arms, but those were minor compared to my back.

They fitted me with this back brace called a TLSO that I have to wear pretty much all the time. It's uncomfortable and makes it hard to do anything normal. I can barely walk without help and definitely can't do any lifting or bending. They referred me to Dr. Hayes, who is a spine specialist, and I saw him on March 18th.

This injury has really turned our lives upside down. I'm the main breadwinner in our family - my wife Carla works part-time at a dental office, but we really depend on my paycheck to pay the bills and take care of our two kids, Emma who is 8 and Diego who is 12. Not being able to work has put a lot of financial stress on us. We're trying to keep up with the mortgage and all our expenses, but it's really hard.

The worst part is not being able to do things with my kids. I can't pick up Emma when she wants a hug, and I can't throw a football with Diego in the backyard like we used to do. Simple things like getting dressed in the morning or taking a shower are really difficult and painful. Carla has to help me with a lot of basic things, which makes me feel helpless.

I'm also worried about my future. I love my job and I'm good at it. I've been doing concrete work for eight years and it's how I support my family. Dr. Hayes says I should be able to go back to work eventually, but I'm scared about whether I'll be able to do the heavy lifting and physical work that concrete finishing requires. What if I re-injure my back? What if I can't do the job the same way I used to?

The guys at work have been really supportive. James has checked on me several times and the company seems to be handling everything properly with the insurance. Carlos stopped by the house to see how I was doing, which meant a lot to me. Even my union rep from Local 826 called to make sure I was getting the help I needed.

I want to be clear that this was a complete accident. Nobody did anything wrong on purpose. The vibrator just malfunctioned for whatever reason - maybe something inside broke or got stuck. I was doing my job the same way I always do it, being careful and following all the safety procedures I've been trained on. I never expected a piece of equipment that I've used hundreds of times to suddenly go haywire like that.

I'm trying to stay positive and follow all the doctor's orders. I take my medications, wear the brace like I'm supposed to, and I'm going to do whatever physical therapy they recommend when the time comes. I want to get better and get back to work as soon as I safely can. My family needs me to be healthy and able to provide for them.

This has been the scariest and most difficult thing that has ever happened to me. I keep thinking about how much worse it could have been - what if I had hit my head or damaged my spinal cord? I feel lucky in some ways, but also really frustrated that this happened at all. All I was doing was my job, using equipment that should have been safe.

I hope this statement helps explain what happened from my point of view. I've tried to remember everything as clearly as I can. If there's anything else you need to know, please let me know. I just want to get through this and get my life back to normal for my family.
