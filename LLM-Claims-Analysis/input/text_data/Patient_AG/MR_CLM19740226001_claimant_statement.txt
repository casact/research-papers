I, AG, was working at the downtown construction site on February 26th, 1974, when the incident occured that changed everything for me. It was a regular day, or at least I thought so, and I was up on the scaffolding doing maintenance work, pretty typical for a construction worker like me. I had my safety harness on, but there was a moment when the scaffolding just... it wasn't stable, like it should've been, and before I knew it, I lost my balance and fell. It all happened so fast and I couldn't believe it. I hit the ground and felt a really, really sharp pain in my left wrist. I heard my wrist crack, and I knew immediately something was really wrong. I also landed awkwardly on my right knee and sprained my left ankle. After the fall, I was in so much pain, it was hard to think straight.

John Doe and Jane Smith were there, and they witnessed the whole thing. I remember them rushing over to help me, but at that moment, I just wanted the pain to stop. I was taken in for my first treatment the next day, where Dr. Richard Lee did an X-ray and confirmed that my left wrist was fractured. I remember the fear of wondering how long it would take to heal and if I could even go back to work. They did surgery on March 5th to fix my wrist and put me in a cast, and I started physical therapy on March 15th.

During my recovery, I was unable to do anything that I normally could, and it really, really hurt me emotionally. Not being able to work meant not only the physical impacts but also the mental toll; construction is my life, it's what I do best, and being sidelined was tough. I couldn\u2019t lift heavy things and could barely do simple tasks around the house, which made me feel useless. I was worried about finances, bills piling up, and how much time I would miss from work. I didn\u2019t return to work normally until April 15th, when I was finally able to do some modified duties.

Even after I got back to work, my life didn\u2019t feel normal. I was constantly worried about my injuries and if I would ever be 'me' again. I felt like I was always limping or favoring my wrist and worried every time I climbed up scaffolding again. My wife noticed how much it affected me, always telling me to take it easy but I didn\u2019t want to let anyone down. By June 1st, I finally felt like I achieved my Maximum Medical Improvement, but the experience really changed me. The pain I endured was not just physical but emotional as well.
