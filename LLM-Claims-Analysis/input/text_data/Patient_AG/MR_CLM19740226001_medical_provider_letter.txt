I am writing to provide a comprehensive medical summary regarding my patient, AG, who sustained injuries on February 26, 1974, while working at a downtown construction site. As per your request, I am detailing the clinical findings, treatment plan, prognosis, and work restrictions for your review.  

**Clinical Summary:**  
AG, a 43-year-old construction worker, presented to our facility on February 27, 1974, with a primary complaint of severe pain and swelling in the left wrist following a fall from scaffolding. Initial examination revealed a fractured left wrist, contusions on the right knee, and a sprained left ankle. An X-ray performed on the same day confirmed the fracture.  

**Examination Findings:**  
Upon physical examination, the following findings were noted:  
- Left wrist: Deformity with limited range of motion (ROM), tenderness, and swelling.  
- Right knee: Bruising with full passive range of motion, no instability.  
- Left ankle: Mild swelling and tenderness, with negative anterior drawer test.  

**Treatment Plan:**  
AG was treated with immobilization of the left wrist using a cast, and pain management was initiated with NSAIDs prescribed prn. On March 5, 1974, AG underwent surgical intervention for the wrist fracture, which included open reduction and internal fixation. Post-operative care included physical therapy (PT) starting March 15, 1974, focusing on regaining strength and mobility.  

**Prognosis:**  
AG has shown significant progress since the initiation of treatment. As of June 1, 1974, he has reached maximum medical improvement (MMI) with a functional capacity to perform modified duties at work. The prognosis for full recovery is good, although some limitations in wrist motion may persist.  

**Work Restrictions:**  
I recommend that AG continues with modified duties, avoiding heavy lifting and repetitive wrist movements for the next 6 weeks. A follow-up evaluation is scheduled for July 15, 1974, to reassess his condition and determine any further work restrictions.
